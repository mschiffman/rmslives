⏳

The "Non-Past" Concept

In Japanese, there is no separate "future" tense structurally built into the verb. The present tense is inherently a "Non-Past" tense. It represents both habitual current actions and future intent.

Example: 食べる (Taberu)

Can translate to: "I eat" (habitual) OR "I will eat" (future).

Context and time adverbs (like ashita - tomorrow) do the heavy lifting to distinguish future intent.

Verb Group Distribution

Understanding Group 1 (U-verbs) rules is critical, as they make up the vast majority of the language.

⚠️ Common Pitfalls

  • Over-complicating Future Do not look for a "will" particle in Japanese. The present tense verb itself handles future intent.
  • Irregular Verbs Mix-up Students often try to conjugate Suru (to do) as a Group 1 verb. It is completely irregular and must be memorized (Suru ➞ Shimasu).
  • Forgetting "Deshita" In the Polite Negative Past, beginners often say ~masen instead of the full ~masen deshita.
