COD

Direct Object

Replaces a noun that receives the action directly — no preposition between verb and object. Placed before the conjugated verb. Use l' before a vowel or silent h.

Je regarde la télé. → Je la regarde.
Il voit ses amis. → Il les voit.
Tu écoutes le prof. → Tu l'écoutes.
COI

Indirect Object

Replaces à + person. Use lui (singular) or leur (plural) — same for both genders. Placed before the conjugated verb.

Je parle à Marie. → Je lui parle.
Elle écrit aux parents. → Elle leur écrit.
⚠ lui/leur do NOT contract before a vowel.
RÉFL.

Reflexive Pronouns

Used with pronominal verbs — the subject acts upon itself. The reflexive pronoun must match the subject. me/te → m'/t' before a vowel.

Je me lève.  (I get up)
Ils se préparent.  (They get ready)
Je m'habille.  (I get dressed)
DISJOINT

Disjunctive (Stressed)

Used after prepositions (pour, avec, chez, sans…), in comparisons, and alone for emphasis. Never used as the main verb subject.

Je vais chez lui.  (his place)
C'est moi.  (It's me.)
Elle est plus grande que toi.

Pronoun Word Order

Object pronouns come immediately before the verb — or before the infinitive when there is one.

Affirmative
Subject + me/te/se/nous/vous + le/la/les + lui/leur + Verb
Negation
Subject ne pronoun(s) Verb pas
Infinitive
Verb₁ + pronoun + Infinitive
Imperative (+)
Verb – le/la/les – moi/toi/lui… (me→moi, te→toi)
Je le lui donne. I give it to him.
Je ne le mange pas. I don't eat it.
Je vais l'acheter. I'm going to buy it.
The Special Case of "On"

In spoken/informal French, on replaces nous to mean "we." The verb always conjugates in the 3rd person singular, even though the meaning is plural.

Register Subject Reflexive Direct Obj. Indirect Obj. Disjunctive
Informal on se nous
Formal nous
Informal → On va au cinéma. = We're going to the movies.
Formal  → Nous allons au cinéma.
Reflexive → On se retrouve à midi. = We meet at noon.

Possessive Adjectives & Pronouns

Key Rule: Possessive adjectives agree with the thing possessed, not the possessor. son livre = his or her book (because livre is masculine). Also use mon/ton/son before any feminine noun starting with a vowel: mon amie.

2. Practice Exercises

I

Subject Pronouns

Rule: Replace the subject in parentheses with the correct pronoun, matching number and gender.
II

Direct Object (COD)

Rule: Replace the underlined noun with le, la, l', les. Place it before the verb.
Tu regardes.
Il finit.
Nous écoutons.
Je aime.
Elle achète.
III

Indirect Object (COI)

Rule: Replace à + person with lui (singular) or leur (plural).
Je écris.
Tu téléphones.
Elle le donne.
Nous envoyons le colis.
Vous répondez.
IV

Reflexive & Special Cases

Rules: Negation: ne + pronoun + verb + pas. Infinitive: pronoun goes before the infinitive. Positive imperative: me → moi.
